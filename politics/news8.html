Had Trump been president, ‘maybe’ there would not have been war in Ukraine, Putin says – as it happened

24 January 2025


                    What started looking like a slow news day ended up being pretty lively! That’s the joy of Europe Live.

But now it’s a wrap from me, Jakub Krupa.

Here are the three things to take away from today’s blog:

Russian president Vladimir Putin suggested that the invasion of Ukraine might have never happened had Donald Trump been US president at the time. His comments echoed similar claims from Trump and could further fuel Kyiv’s concerns about the pair potentially attempting to hastily strike a peace deal without involving Ukrainian and European leaders in the talks (16:59).

Around 625,000 homes, farms, and businesses in Ireland remain without electricity this evening, RTÉ reports, as a result of Storm Éowyn, with widespread disruptions to travel. Follow our weather live blog for the latest.

Slovakia’s political crisis was deepening on Friday night as tens of thousands took to the streets in Bratislava to protest against Robert Fico’s government amid growing concerns about the country’s pro-Russia foreign policy (18:32).

And I leave you with a reminder that Belarus will hold presidential elections this Sunday as “Europe’s last dictator,” longtime president and Putin ally Alexander Lukashenko, is set to extend his 31-year reign.

The EU called it “a sham” (12:43), and Sviatlana Tsikhanouskaya, the head of the exiled Belarusian opposition, said “what in the democratic world you call elections has nothing in common with this event in Belarus” (11:33).

That’s all from me, Jakub Krupa, for today. Have a great weekend, and see you on Monday!
