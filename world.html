Severe storm lashes parts of UK and Ireland, causing travel chaos :

A rare “stay at home” warning has been issued for parts of the United Kingdom and Ireland as a severe storm lashes the region, bringing dangerous 100mph (160 kmh) winds and unleashing travel chaos. Storm Éowyn, an extratropical “bomb” cyclone that has formed in the North Atlantic and intensified rapidly, has hit rail services, delayed flights and forced road closures. The strongest winds and most significant impacts are expected in Northern Ireland and central and southwestern parts of Scotland, according to the UK's Met Office. A red weather warning was established Friday morning local time in Northern Ireland and parts of Scotland.
